Editorial snapshot
Bali earns every superlative. The combination of Hindu temple culture, dramatic volcanic landscapes, world-class surf, and some of Southeast Asia's most exciting food makes it genuinely hard to rival. The catch: avoid July, August, and the Christmas–New Year window unless you book everything months in advance. Outside peak season, Bali offers extraordinary variety at a fraction of what you'd pay elsewhere in the world.

Food & Drink

What to eat in Bali

Balinese food is distinct from the rest of Indonesia — deeply spiced, rich in ceremony, and eaten at every hour of day.

01
Babi Guling (Suckling Pig)
Bali's most celebrated dish — a whole pig slow-roasted over coconut husks with turmeric, lemongrass, galangal, and shrimp paste. Ibu Oka in Ubud is the most famous spot; arrive before noon as it sells out.
02
Nasi Campur
Bali's everyday staple — steamed rice served with small portions of multiple dishes: spit-roasted meat, sautéed vegetables, crispy tempeh, sambal, and peanuts. Every warung has its own version.
03
Bebek Betutu (Slow-Cooked Duck)
Duck marinated in a complex spice paste, wrapped in banana leaves, and slow-cooked for up to 8 hours. A ceremonial dish you'll find at better warungs in Ubud and Seminyak.
04
Sate Lilit
Uniquely Balinese satay — minced seafood or chicken mixed with shredded coconut and spices, wrapped around lemongrass sticks and grilled. Sold at roadside stalls for pennies; unmissable.
05
Kopi Bali (Balinese Coffee)
Strong, unfiltered, and served sweet. Bali grows its own arabica in the Kintamani highlands — drink it at a warung above the Tegallalang terraces for the full experience.

Money-saving tip
Eat at local warungs (family-run cafés) rather than tourist restaurants and your food costs drop by 60–70%. A full plate of nasi campur costs IDR 20,000–35,000 (~$1.20–2.10). Alcohol is the biggest budget-buster — imported spirits are taxed heavily in Indonesia.

Getting Around

Transport in Bali

Bali has no public rail or bus network. Getting around requires planning — but the options are affordable and straightforward.

TransportBest forApprox costNotes
Grab / GojekShort trips in south BaliIDR 15,000–60,000Cheapest and most transparent. Note: ride-hailing is banned outside the south.
Scooter rentalUbud, rural areasIDR 70,000–100,000 / dayMost flexible. International driving licence required. Traffic in Canggu can be intense.
Private driverFull-day tours, north BaliIDR 500,000–700,000 / dayBest value for multi-stop days. Drivers often double as excellent local guides.
Metered taxi (Blue Bird)Airport, Kuta, Nusa DuaIDR 50,000–150,000Use Blue Bird only — clearly marked. Avoid unlicensed taxis at the airport.
Perama shuttleKuta–Ubud–Lovina routesIDR 80,000–150,000Tourist shuttle network. Slower but cheap for longer inter-area journeys.

  • ✓Get an Indonesian SIM at the airport (Telkomsel or XL) — data is cheap and Maps/Grab won't work without it.
  • ✓Traffic around Canggu and Seminyak is severe 4–7pm. Plan your afternoon accordingly.
  • ✓Ubud to south Bali takes 1.5–2 hours by car — plan day trips with this in mind.

Practical Info

Before you go — Bali essentials

The quick facts every visitor to Bali needs before they land.

🛂
Entry
Visa on Arrival
Most nationalities receive a 30-day VoA for IDR 500,000 (~$30), extendable once. Apply at the VoA counter before immigration — or use the e-VoA online before departure.
💱
Money
Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)
Cash is still king in Bali. Withdraw IDR from ATMs (BCA and BNI charge the lowest fees). Avoid street money changers — use authorised booths at the airport or in town.
🩺
Health
Health & Safety
Drink bottled water only. Bali belly is common — avoid ice at street stalls. Dengue fever is present year-round; use mosquito repellent at dusk. Travel insurance is essential.
🛕
Culture
Temple Etiquette
A sarong and sash must be worn at all Hindu temples — most loan them free. Always walk clockwise around a temple. Never step on offerings placed on the ground.
📶
Connectivity
SIM & Internet
SIM cards sold inside arrivals at DPS. Telkomsel offers the best rural coverage. A 10GB data pack costs ~IDR 50,000 (~$3). Wi-Fi widely available in tourist areas.
🌡️
Climate
Temperature
Hot and humid year-round, 27–32°C on the coast. Ubud and highlands are cooler at 20–25°C. Pack light clothing, a rain jacket for wet season, and high-SPF sunscreen.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions about Bali

Most nationalities receive a Visa on Arrival (VoA) for IDR 500,000 (~$30), valid for 30 days and extendable once. You can also apply for the e-VoA online before travel. Always verify current requirements at imigrasi.go.id.
Bali is one of Asia's most solo-friendly destinations. Violent crime is rare. Watch for scooter theft (use a chain lock), unlicensed taxi touts at the airport, and street money exchange scams. Solo female travellers report feeling very safe throughout.
Budget travellers can get by on $30–45/day (hostel, local food, scooter). A comfortable mid-range trip costs $80–130/day. Luxury villas and private drivers push this to $250+/day. Bali is excellent value across all budget levels compared to Western destinations.
