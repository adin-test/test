About Buy Now Pay Later Flights with Klarna

Klarna is one of the most widely recognised buy now pay later providers in the world, operating across dozens of countries and currencies, from the US and UK to Sweden, Japan, and Brazil. Founded in Stockholm, it has grown into a global payments platform that lets shoppers spread the cost of purchases at checkout, and flights are no exception. On Laters, booking a flight with Klarna works like this: search for your route, select Klarna at checkout, and choose the plan that suits your budget. Your e-ticket is issued once payment is approved. Depending on where you are, you may be able to split the fare into four biweekly instalments, defer the full balance for 30 days, or spread the cost over several months through a financing plan. Some plans carry no interest when paid on time; others are interest-bearing credit products subject to a credit check and approval. Klarna is a credit product. In the UK, Klarna's Pay in 3 and Pay in 30 days are regulated by the FCA as Deferred Payment Credit, and Klarna Financial Services UK Limited (FRN 987889) is authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority. Borrowing responsibly means only committing to instalments you can genuinely afford. Missed payments can result in late fees, may be reported to credit reference agencies, and may affect your credit standing and your ability to borrow in future. Visit www.klarna.com for current terms, eligibility criteria, and country-specific plan availability.

FAQ

FAQs: Using Klarna to Book Flights

Everything you need to know about paying for flights with Klarna on Laters.

Yes, you can book flights through Laters.com and pay using Klarna as a credit option at checkout. Klarna offers several plans including Pay in 4, Pay in 30 days, monthly financing, and Pay in full today, depending on your country and eligibility. Because these are credit products, approval is not guaranteed and a credit check may apply. Visit https://www.klarna.com for the most current terms and eligibility criteria.

Your e-ticket is issued once your Klarna payment has been approved and the booking is confirmed by the airline. You do not need to wait until all instalments are paid the ticket is tied to the booking confirmation, not the final payment. If you have not received your e-ticket within the expected timeframe, check your email spam folder or contact Laters.com support.

Klarna's Pay in 4 and Pay in 30 days plans typically involve a soft credit check, which does not affect your credit score. The monthly financing plan (Pay over time) is subject to a credit check as it is an interest-bearing credit product with an APR ranging from 0.00% to 35.99%. For full details on how Klarna assesses eligibility, visit https://www.klarna.com.

If you miss a payment on Klarna's Pay in 4 plan, a late fee of up to $7 may be charged. Missed payments on the monthly financing plan could result in additional costs and may be reported to credit reference agencies, potentially affecting your credit score. Klarna may also restrict your ability to use the service for future purchases. Always review the full terms at https://www.klarna.com before committing to a plan.

For higher-value long-haul fares, Klarna's Pay over time (monthly financing) plan is worth considering, as it spreads the cost over 6, 12, 18, or 24 months. However, this plan carries an APR of 0.00% to 35.99% and is subject to credit approval, so the total cost will depend on the rate you are offered. Pay in 4 is capped at a $1,000 basket, so it may not cover premium long-haul fares in full. Check https://www.klarna.com for current basket limits and financing terms in your country.

Klarna is available across a broad range of countries, including the US, UK, Germany, Sweden, Australia, Canada, France, the Netherlands, Singapore, Japan, and many more. However, the plans available vary by country for example, Australian travellers currently have access to Pay in 4 and Pay in full, while US travellers can access all four plans. Check the country availability section on this page or visit https://www.klarna.com to confirm which plans are offered where you are.

You can use Klarna for premium cabin fares, though plan availability depends on the basket size. Klarna's Pay in 4 plan has a maximum basket of $1,000, which may not cover the full cost of many business or first class tickets. For higher fares, the monthly financing plan may be more suitable, subject to credit approval and an APR of 0.00% to 35.99%. Confirm current basket limits at https://www.klarna.com before booking.

If your flight is cancelled and a refund is processed by the airline or Laters.com, the refund is typically returned to your Klarna account and applied against your outstanding balance. If the refund exceeds what you have paid so far, Klarna will generally return the remainder to your original payment method. Processing times can vary, so contact Klarna directly or check https://www.klarna.com for guidance on how refunds are handled for your specific plan.

Using Klarna's Pay in 4 or Pay in 30 days plans typically involves only a soft credit check, which is not visible to other lenders and does not affect your credit score. The monthly financing (Pay over time) plan involves a more formal credit assessment, and missed payments on any plan could be reported to credit bureaus, which may negatively impact your score. Making all payments on time is the best way to avoid any adverse effect. For full details, visit https://www.klarna.com.

Klarna's Pay in 30 days plan carries no interest and no late fees, provided you pay the full balance within 30 days of purchase. Paying nothing upfront means the full fare is deferred, and you remain liable for that balance on the due date. If you do not pay in full by the deadline, Klarna may refer the debt to a collections process, which could affect your credit standing. Always confirm the current terms for your country at https://www.klarna.com.
